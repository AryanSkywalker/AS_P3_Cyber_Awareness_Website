What is it?

Also referred to as internet security awareness, cyber security awareness is considered the first and formost step in achieving true cyber-security. It refers to how much the end-users know about the threats their network via which they exist in the world of internet face, the risk they introduce and mitigating security best practices to guide their behaviour. Cybersecurity helps protect computer systems from unauthorized use, theft, harm et cetera.

What is it's need?

In computer-network theory, the end-users are deemed the most vulnerable and weakest link in a network. Due to this natural yet dangerous aspect of networking, it has become very important to be aware of potential threats that users face while surfing the internet and sharing important data via networks. This would help individuals and organizations secure their place on the internet and store and retrieve vital data off of the internet safely.

Celebrating Cybersecurity...

Every October since that of 2003, is recognized and observed as Cybersecurity Awareness Month. Though it was an initiative undertaken by american security agencies, today the cause for which it was started is supported in almost every country of the world, including India. Throughout the 4 to 5 weeks of October, organizations and individuals train themselves in becoming aware of dangers of cyberthreats and how to carefully evade them.

What danger does cybercrime pose?

Cybercrime is the use of a computer to indulge or take part in illegal activity like, but not limited to, fraud, identity theft and privacy violation et cetera. With an increasing number of cybercrimes being committed every year, the risk of not educating yourself on cybersecurity continues to grow. While it is impossible to classify every crime committed on the internet coherently, they can boil down to:

Theft of data
Stealing data from unauthorized places such as personal photos from phones or military secrets from government computers
Vandalism
Destroying vital records in banks or personal data off of computers
Fraud
Usually involves channeling funds through unfair ways
Invasion of privacy
Illegalling entering protected personal, financial or medical databases and gathering information.
